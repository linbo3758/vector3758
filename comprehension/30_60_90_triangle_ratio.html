30-60-90 Triangle Ratio
Step 1 of 3
The Equilateral Source
We start with a perfect Equilateral Triangle. By definition:
• All three side lengths are equal to \(2\).
• All three interior angles are exactly \(60^\circ\).
Next, we split this equilateral triangle in half by drawing a straight perpendicular altitude line down the middle.
This bisection creates key geometric transformations:
• The base is cut perfectly in half, from \(2\) units into \(1\) unit.
• The top angle is bisected, from \(60^\circ\) into \(30^\circ\).
• The line forms a perpendicular \(90^\circ\) right angle at the base.
Now, look only at the remaining right-angled half. We know the base is \(1\) and the hypotenuse is \(2\). Let's solve for the height (\(h\)) using the Pythagorean Theorem:
\[a^2 + b^2 = c^2\] \[1^2 + h^2 = 2^2\] \[1 + h^2 = 4 \implies h^2 = 3\] \[h = \sqrt{3}\]
Therefore, the three sides of this \(30^\circ-60^\circ-90^\circ\) triangle match the absolute ratio: \[1 : \sqrt{3} : 2\]
